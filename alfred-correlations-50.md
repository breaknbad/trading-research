## STOCK MARKET CORRELATIONS (1–25)

**#1: Yield Curve Inversion → Recession/Bear Market**
Direction: Market Down
Correlation: ~85%
Mechanism: When the 2Y yield exceeds the 10Y yield, it signals banks tightening lending and an economic slowdown ahead — every US recession since 1960 was preceded by an inversion. The lag is 6–24 months.
Action: After inversion, begin shifting to defensive sectors (utilities, healthcare, staples). Increase cash/short exposure once the curve re-steepens (un-inversion often marks the start of recession).

**#2: Copper/Gold Ratio Rising**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~80%
Mechanism: Copper is an industrial demand proxy; gold is a fear proxy. A rising ratio signals economic expansion expectations outpacing risk aversion, which aligns with equity strength.
Action: Go long equities (especially cyclicals/industrials) when the ratio breaks above its 200-day moving average.

**#3: AAII Bearish Sentiment >55% (Contrarian)**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~82%
Mechanism: When retail investors are overwhelmingly bearish, most selling has already occurred. Markets are a discounting mechanism — extreme fear historically precedes 6-month rallies.
Action: Buy S&P 500 index exposure when AAII bears exceed 55%. Hold 3–6 months.

**#4: ISM Manufacturing PMI Crossing Above 50**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~83%
Mechanism: The 50 level separates expansion from contraction. Crossing above signals manufacturing recovery, which leads corporate earnings higher with a 1–2 quarter lag.
Action: Go long industrials and small caps when PMI crosses above 50 from below.

**#5: Initial Jobless Claims 4-Week Average Trending Lower**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~78%
Mechanism: Declining claims indicate a strengthening labor market, which supports consumer spending (70% of GDP). Equities follow earnings, and earnings follow consumer health.
Action: Stay long equities when the 4-week average is declining and below 300K. Reduce exposure when it spikes 15%+ from trough.

**#6: US Dollar Index (DXY) Declining**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~76%
Mechanism: A weaker dollar boosts multinational earnings (translation effect), makes US assets cheaper for foreign buyers, and eases global financial conditions since much debt is dollar-denominated.
Action: Overweight equities (especially emerging markets and large-cap multinationals) when DXY breaks below its 50-day MA.

**#7: Put/Call Ratio Spike Above 1.2 (Contrarian)**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~79%
Mechanism: Extreme put buying signals panic hedging. When everyone is already hedged, the marginal flow shifts to buying. Historically a reliable 1–4 week mean-reversion signal.
Action: Buy SPY calls or go long when the equity put/call ratio exceeds 1.2 on a 5-day average. Target a 2–4 week hold.

**#8: Insider Buying Clusters**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~80%
Mechanism: Corporate insiders buy their own stock when they believe it's undervalued relative to private knowledge of business fundamentals. Clusters of buying across sectors signal broad market undervaluation.
Action: Track SEC Form 4 filings. When insider buy/sell ratio spikes, go long both individual names and broad indices.

**#9: VIX Term Structure in Backwardation**
Direction: Both (short-term down, then up)
Correlation: ~77%
Mechanism: When near-term VIX futures exceed longer-dated ones, it signals acute fear. This is unsustainable — markets either crash quickly then recover, or the fear itself marks the bottom.
Action: Wait for backwardation to flip back to contango, then buy aggressively. Alternatively, sell VIX call spreads once backwardation exceeds 10%.

**#10: Semiconductor Index (SOX) Leading S&P 500**
Direction: Both
Correlation: ~81%
Mechanism: Semis are the picks-and-shovels of the modern economy. Their order books reflect demand 6–12 months ahead. SOX typically leads the S&P 500 by 2–4 months at turning points.
Action: Use SOX relative strength vs SPX as a leading signal. If SOX breaks to new highs first, buy SPX. If SOX diverges lower, reduce exposure.

**#11: NYSE Advance-Decline Line Divergence**
Direction: Market Down
Correlation: ~84%
Mechanism: When the S&P 500 makes new highs but fewer stocks participate (A/D line diverges lower), it means a handful of mega-caps are masking broad weakness. Narrow rallies eventually fail.
Action: When A/D line makes lower highs while SPX makes higher highs over 2+ months, begin reducing long exposure and adding hedges.

**#12: High-Yield Credit Spreads Widening**
Direction: Market Down
Correlation: ~85%
Mechanism: The junk bond market prices default risk. Widening spreads signal deteriorating credit conditions and risk aversion — this typically leads equity selloffs by 1–3 months.
Action: When HY OAS spreads widen 100+ bps from a trough over 4–8 weeks, reduce equity exposure. Use HYG/JNK relative to LQD as a proxy.

**#13: M2 Money Supply Growth Accelerating**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~78%
Mechanism: More money chasing assets inflates prices. When central banks expand liquidity, equities are a primary beneficiary. M2 growth leads stock returns by roughly 6 months.
Action: Track YoY M2 growth. When it inflects positive after a contraction, go long. When it peaks and declines, start reducing.

**#14: Transportation Index (Dow Transports) Confirming**
Direction: Both
Correlation: ~76%
Mechanism: Dow Theory — if industrials make new highs and transports confirm, the economy is genuinely expanding (goods are being made AND shipped). Non-confirmation is a warning.
Action: Stay long when both indices trend together. Hedge when transports diverge from industrials for 4+ weeks.

**#15: Housing Starts Rising**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~77%
Mechanism: Housing is the most interest-rate-sensitive sector and has enormous multiplier effects (appliances, furniture, labor, materials). Rising starts signal consumer confidence and credit availability.
Action: Overweight homebuilders, materials, and consumer discretionary when housing starts trend above their 12-month average.

**#16: Oil Price Shock (>40% in <6 Months)**
Direction: Market Down
Correlation: ~80%
Mechanism: Sudden oil spikes act as a tax on consumers and compress corporate margins (especially transportation, manufacturing). Most recessions since 1970 were preceded by an oil price shock.
Action: Reduce equity exposure and overweight energy when crude spikes >40% in under 6 months. Rotate back once oil stabilizes or declines.

**#17: CEO Confidence Survey Rising**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~79%
Mechanism: CEOs control capex and hiring decisions. When their confidence rises, capital investment follows, driving earnings growth and economic expansion.
Action: Use the Conference Board CEO Confidence Index as a 6-month leading signal. Go long cyclicals when it inflects upward.

**#18: Margin Debt Declining from Record Highs**
Direction: Market Down
Correlation: ~78%
Mechanism: Peak margin debt means maximum leverage in the system. When deleveraging begins, forced selling creates cascading price declines. Margin debt peaks have preceded every major bear market.
Action: Track NYSE margin debt. When it drops 10%+ from a record high, adopt a defensive posture. Don't short — use it as a reduce-exposure signal.

**#19: January Barometer**
Direction: Both
Correlation: ~75%
Mechanism: "As January goes, so goes the year." A positive January has preceded a positive full year ~75% of the time since 1950. Likely reflects institutional allocation decisions at year-start.
Action: If January closes positive, stay long for the year. If negative, maintain a more cautious/hedged posture.

**#20: "Sell in May" Seasonal Pattern**
Direction: Both
Correlation: ~76%
Mechanism: November–April historically outperforms May–October. Likely driven by bonus-driven institutional flows in Q1, lower summer liquidity, and fiscal year-end patterns.
Action: Overweight equities November–April, shift to more defensive/cash May–October. Not a hard rule — use with other signals.

**#21: Fed Funds Rate Cuts Beginning**
Direction: Market Up (6–12 months later)
Correlation: ~82%
Mechanism: Rate cuts ease financial conditions, reduce corporate borrowing costs, and make equities more attractive vs bonds. The first cut often coincides with economic slowdown, but markets are forward-looking.
Action: After the first rate cut, begin building equity exposure. Historically, the S&P is up 12 months after the first cut ~80% of the time (unless a severe recession).

**#22: Global Central Bank Balance Sheets Expanding**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~83%
Mechanism: When the Fed, ECB, BOJ, and PBOC collectively expand balance sheets, global liquidity surges. This is the single most powerful macro force for asset prices — liquidity finds its way into equities.
Action: Track the combined balance sheet of the Big 4 central banks. Go long risk assets during expansion; reduce during contraction.

**#23: Consumer Confidence (Michigan) Dropping Below 60**
Direction: Market Up (Contrarian)
Correlation: ~76%
Mechanism: Extreme consumer pessimism, like AAII, tends to mark bottoms. By the time the average consumer feels terrible, asset prices have already discounted bad news.
Action: When University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment drops below 60, begin accumulating equity positions for a 6–12 month horizon.

**#24: Small-Cap Relative Strength (Russell 2000/S&P 500 Ratio Rising)**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~78%
Mechanism: Small caps are more domestically focused and credit-sensitive. When they outperform, it signals improving domestic economic conditions, healthy credit markets, and broad risk appetite.
Action: When IWM/SPY ratio turns up from a trough, go long small caps and increase overall equity exposure.

**#25: S&P 500 Golden Cross (50-day MA crosses above 200-day MA)**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~78%
Mechanism: This trend-following signal captures the transition from bear to bull. While lagging, it filters out bear market rallies and keeps you on the right side of the major trend.
Action: Go long on the golden cross; reduce/exit on the death cross (50 crosses below 200). Works best on weekly timeframes to reduce whipsaws.

## CRYPTO MARKET CORRELATIONS (26–50)

**#26: Bitcoin Exchange Net Outflows**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~80%
Mechanism: When BTC flows off exchanges to cold storage, it reduces available supply for selling. Sustained outflows signal accumulation by long-term holders who aren't planning to sell.
Action: Track net exchange flows via Glassnode/CryptoQuant. Sustained outflows (7-day average) = bullish. Go long or hold.

**#27: Stablecoin Dominance Declining**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~79%
Mechanism: When stablecoin market cap as % of total crypto declines, it means capital is rotating from stablecoins into risk assets (BTC, ETH, alts). It's a direct measure of crypto risk appetite.
Action: Go long BTC/ETH when stablecoin dominance drops below its 30-day moving average.

**#28: Bitcoin Funding Rates Extremely Negative**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~82%
Mechanism: Negative funding means perpetual futures traders are overwhelmingly short and paying longs to hold. This creates a short-squeeze setup — shorts become the fuel for a rally.
Action: Go long BTC spot when aggregated funding rates are deeply negative (<-0.03%) across major exchanges. Target 1–2 week hold.

**#29: Bitcoin Funding Rates Extremely Positive (>0.1%)**
Direction: Market Down
Correlation: ~78%
Mechanism: Excessive positive funding means leveraged longs are paying shorts. The market is overcrowded long on leverage — any dip triggers cascading liquidations.
Action: Reduce long exposure or open short hedges when funding exceeds 0.1% on a sustained basis.

**#30: Bitcoin Hash Rate Making New Highs**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~77%
Mechanism: Rising hash rate means miners are investing in infrastructure, signaling confidence in future BTC prices. It also increases network security, which supports fundamental value.
Action: Use hash rate trend as a background bullish signal. If hash rate is rising while price is falling, it's an accumulation opportunity.

**#31: Whale Wallet Accumulation (1K+ BTC Addresses Growing)**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~80%
Mechanism: Large holders have asymmetric information and longer time horizons. When whale addresses increase their holdings, they're signaling conviction. They tend to accumulate before major moves up.
Action: Track whale address count and balance changes on-chain. Accumulation phases = go long with a 3–6 month horizon.

**#32: Bitcoin MVRV Z-Score Below 0**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~85%
Mechanism: MVRV Z-Score compares market cap to realized cap (cost basis of all coins). Below 0 means the average holder is underwater — historically, this has marked every major cycle bottom.
Action: Aggressively accumulate BTC when MVRV Z-Score drops below 0. This is a generational buy signal.

**#33: Bitcoin MVRV Z-Score Above 7**
Direction: Market Down
Correlation: ~83%
Mechanism: Extremely high MVRV means massive unrealized profits across the network. Holders are incentivized to sell, creating overwhelming supply pressure.
Action: Begin scaling out of positions and taking profits when MVRV Z-Score exceeds 7. Full exit by 8+.

**#34: Stablecoin Supply on Exchanges Rising**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~78%
Mechanism: Stablecoins on exchanges represent dry powder — capital parked and ready to buy. Rising stablecoin reserves signal incoming buying pressure.
Action: Go long when exchange stablecoin reserves trend up while prices are flat or down (accumulation phase).

**#35: DeFi Total Value Locked (TVL) Expanding**

Correlation: ~76%
Mechanism: Rising TVL means capital is being deployed into crypto-native yield strategies rather than exiting. It signals ecosystem confidence and increases demand for collateral assets (ETH, SOL, etc.).
Action: Track TVL via DefiLlama. Rising TVL + rising prices = confirmed uptrend. Rising TVL + flat prices = bullish divergence.

Mechanism: Halving cuts new BTC supply by 50%. With constant or growing demand, reduced supply creates a supply shock. The full effect takes 12–18 months to play out as the supply deficit compounds.
Action: Accumulate BTC 3–6 months before halving. Hold through 12–18 months post-halving. Begin taking profits when euphoria becomes mainstream.

Mechanism: New USDT minting directly adds liquidity to crypto markets. Tether mints when there is demand for dollar-denominated crypto liquidity — it's a real-time measure of capital entering the ecosystem.

Direction: Market Up (from support)

Mechanism: Realized price is the average cost basis of all BTC on-chain. It acts as a psychological and economic support level — the aggregate market "breaks even" here. Drops below it are historically rare and brief.

Correlation: ~76%
Mechanism: Despite "uncorrelated asset" narratives, BTC has been highly correlated with risk assets since 2020 institutional adoption. In liquidity crises, correlations go to 1 — everything sells together.
Action: Reduce crypto exposure when S&P 500 enters a confirmed downtrend (below 200-day MA). The correlation is strongest during risk-off regimes.

**#50: Bitcoin NVT Signal (Network Value to Transactions) Dropping**
Direction: Market Up
Correlation: ~76%

Action: Go long BTC when NVT Signal drops below 45. Trim when it exceeds 150 (overvalued relative to on-chain activity).
